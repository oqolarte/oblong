## Hustling beyond hassle

I stumbled upon [a video about hustling](https://vt.tiktok.com/ZSHJo6eHj/) (TikTok). The vlog (because it
*is* a vlog) was about the strategies the author would do to get back on
tracks if he would need to start all over again at the age
of 44 years old.

### Some initial assumptions:

- single and no kids
- cishet male
- Sleep is 9 hours
- Work is 9 hours (8 hours of work, with 1 hour break)
- Travel time is 1 hour (feasible if night shift since there is
  little to no traffic)
- Me-time is 2 hours
- "Free time" is 3 hours

### And now, the "strategies"

1. **Look for a job in a call center.** Call centers are always hiring.
   Most Filipinos are exposed to English early in their education, so
   working for North America-based clients would be easier. But the work
   will be night shift. This means that social life, if any at all,
   would be limited to fellow workers in the same shift.
2. **Live with parents**. Contribute to the household expenses. Nothing
   wrong with that. Of course, this assumes that everyone is okay with
   this setup. We must also factor who is in charge of what chores,
   which bills to pay, and who sleeps where and for how long.
3. If living with parents is not possible, **rent a bedspace**.
4. **Save 30-40% of monthly salary for 3 months.**
5. **Buy a decent Android phone.** Unclear, though, what constitutes
   "decent" and whether secondhand is fine.
6. **Learn to sell memorial plots and insurance.** Attend trainings, and
   convince co-workers to set meetings with their parents. (At around
   here is where I start losing the plot.)
7. **Learn more about sales and practice them.** Set up appointments
   before and after the shift, assuming of course, such appointments, if
   they materialize, would fit within the 3 hours of "free time". This
   way, the author thinks, he could grow his network through referrals.
   He did not explain how, but to be fair, getting into details was not
   the point.
8. **Look into buying and selling stuff online.** Check out trending
   products on Amazon Prime, and resell them on FB Marketplace at a
   "huge margin" if they are not available here in the Philippines. This
   means he is okay with Amazon even though its existence is
   harmful to society overall. (Check Richard Stallman's page for a
   comprehensive list of [reasons not to buy from Amazon](https://stallman.org/amazon.html).)
9. **Study [AI](/ai) and learn how to "train" Gen X and Boomers**,
   because instead of teaching them to be wary of its pitfalls and to be
   more critical of its impacts in
   society, we need these demographics to generate slop at the speed
   of thought.
10. **Get profit.** The author thinks he could have a 6-digit income (in
    Philippines Pesos) from strategies 1 to 9 in 18 months, assuming of
    course he has not yet destroyed his physical and mental health in
    the process.
11. Bonus: **sell vegetables and/or frozen meat in wet market** from
    the capital generated from previous strategies.

### Some more things that I think the author overlooked:

- Working full time at night shift at mid-40s may not be sustainable.
- There was no focus on personal wellbeing. Burnout imminent.
- None of the strategies seem to involve connnecting and working with
  local communities. At least, it is not explicitly stated.

## Is there an alternative to the grind?

[The Grind exhausts me](/anti-work), even just this second-hand exposure to it, and a
thought experiment at that.

I do not necessarily fault people, especially the working class, for not
having the mental bandwidth to imagine an
[alternate universe](/revolution) bereft of
[profit incentives](/capitalism),
[hierarchy](/hierarchy), and domination of nature.
But I believe it is still our fault as a society that only through this
growth mindset could we imagine a life worth living. Only in continuous
participation in consumerism---and, thus, slow societal
[collapse](/collapse)---do we envision a "comfortable" life, of course,
at the expense of others'.

It is only human to desire, for a higher purpose than whatever all this
is.

So why not desire for better living conditions not only at the
individual level, but also at the communal level?
