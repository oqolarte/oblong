## Thoughts on *God Emperor of Dune*

After months of on-and-off reading, I finally finished *God Emperor of
Dune*, the fourth installment in Dune Saga by Frank Herbert.

It is the most interesting for me in the series so far. If the concept
of sandworms is not already interesting enough, how about a human fusing
with a sandworm---yes, flesh and all---to rule the known universe?

{{< figure src="/image/leto2.jpg" alt="A screencaption of a tweet by the account @pyrameadhead. It is a picture of a man wrapped around by thick blankets of grey and beige, from head to toe, revealing only the face and hair. The man is somehow able to walk on a fashion runway. The caption of the tweet is: Leto II on his way to greet Duncan Idaho 23" caption="Not accurate; not to scale" >}}

Meme aside, the book traverses millenia of [tyranny](/authoritarianism).
The reader is often within the intimate spaces of the despot. But it
does not necessarily mean that proximity grants full understanding of
the all *Why*s. The titular God Emperor, through Herbert's prose, is his
own court jester when it comes to wordplays, albeit the unfunny
and confusing kind.
