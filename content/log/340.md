In a way, I am unlucky to have come across *and* resonated with radical
ideas, those very different from what the dominant systems have
tried and succeeded at inculcating in us. I am unlucky because I must
now wrestle with the comfort of privilege (an effect of lifetime of
exposure to said systems and benefitting from some parts of it) and the
fact the there is no going back to "Ignorance is bliss," now that I am
more aware, politically speaking.

*Wrestle* is a weird term to use here. Comfort has its own headlock
that is hard to escape from. Challenging comfort, even with the promise
and romance of Radical Ideas, means choosing to expose oneself to a
constant, initially low-level trauma. It fulfills a different need, though:
the need to be free, in all sense of the word, as our ancestors had been
free.

However, maintaining comfort in the information age is not so simple. It
still requires having a job, one that churns endless tasks. Currently,
my job requires me to organize a deluge of information. The cognitive
load and emotional burden caused by open action items---I feel them now,
even when I should be shutting them away when "out of hours of
operation." This is the cost of comfort.
