## Preamble

An authoritarian regime is a result of systemic consolidation of power,
which is put to place by compliance of majority, usually those who have
the privilege to just "let things be" because (they think) they have
nothing to fear.

## How it starts

It starts not all at once, but incrementally, while everyone is sure
that the oppression cannot happen "here."

People who are either vying for or already are in power promise a host
of things which would appeal to a lot of people. Then, they will
[create a shared enemy](/fascism)---drugs, terrorism, immigrants, devotees of
another [religion](/religion), believers of a different political ideology, etc.---to
which the followers could direct their collective anger and frustration.
Coincidentally, this developing regime also promises to "address" this
enemey, to make it go away, to put an end to its existence, so long as
the followers continue to comply, knowingly or otherwise.

## This "Obey"

Obedience to so-called [authority](/authority) slowly but surely
constructs the foundation, not [violence](/violence). Although violence
will surely come later when it is too late. To obey is to believe.
Making people believe, then, becomes a long and winding undertaking of
myth-making. The best people to market their ideology to the most number
of individuals are able to convince them to join their cause. Lure them
with promises. Lie if they have to, so long as it gets people to obey in
advance.

People think about dictatorship as military people marching down the
streets, kicking down doors, but at that point, the psychological
conditioning has been done. We would have already obeyed.

States only need enough people trained to obey, stay silent, and
self-censor. Once people become afraid to speak honestly lest they lose
their bank account, platform, job, or reputation, the authoritarian
regime already exists in the mind.

## Control through surveillance

Modern authoritarianism need not look scary to most people.

[Capitalists](/capitalism) are enabling it by promoting
[convenience](/convenience).

States are packaging it as fighting disinformation and "protecting the
children."

One might think that [surveillance](/surveillance) technology begins at
[advertising](/no-ads) as marketers race to get more data about you, to
know you more, to sell you what you think you want. But in a Venn
diagram of surveillance tech and ad tech, the overlap between the two is
a full circle. Through surveillance, governments use it to control their
population, or in more disturbing cases, control other nation's
population.

The technological infrastructure for authoritarianism now exists in a
way it never has before in [human history](/human-history). Digital IDs,
centralized platforms, biometric databases,
[CBDCs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_bank_digital_currency), AI
surveillance, social media monitoring---all of it creates the possibility
for instant, automated control over billions of people simultaneously.
And once those systems exist, [eventually someone will use them maliciously](/threat-actor).
